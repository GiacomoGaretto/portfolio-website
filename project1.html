Digital Forest

Four years of human categorization of trail cam footage

Trail cams are motion-triggered devices used to record animals in their natural habitats. Initially designed for research and wildlife monitoring, these videos have become popular content on social media platforms. Once published, animals are transformed into digital entities, recontextualized within the online ecosystem.

The project examines 273 trail cam videos shared on Italian social platforms (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube) between 2021 and 2024. Hashtags typed into posted video descriptions were considered tools of human categorization.

The installation is divided into two layers of meaning: the animal as a digital object and an inhabitant of a digital forest. These layers are physically reflected through two front screens and a projected backdrop. The top screen shows more than two hours of trail cam footage with its original audio. The lower screen shows data, such as platform, publication date, time, and user.

The projection behind the structure displays a network of hashtags linked to the animals in the footage, with an audio overlay combining sounds from all videos of the currently featured animal.

This creates a growing network that mirrors the evolution of a digital forest, blending human perceptions and animal presence over time.

The structure was custom-built using supports that allowed for great customization, enabling the installation of two screens on the front and housing the RaspBerry board and speakers at the back.
