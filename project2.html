Falken's Room

The World of Arcade Games in a Thrilling 3D Interactive Installation

This thesis was carried out with the aim of analyzing, through the creation of a practical case study, the characteristics of interactive installations, highlighting their development path, communicative potential and critical issues. The case study was the design of a three-dimensional interactive installation centered on the world of arcade video games from the 80s, set up and used by the public on the occasion of the Graphic Days 2022 festival.

Through this project, I aim to explore new communication methods by creatively and innovatively utilizing available technologies. My installation serves as a fusion of play and learning, a space for discovery and connection between generations through the shared experience of gaming. For adults, revisiting 80s arcade games is a nostalgic journey that bridges the generational gap, while for children, it’s an opportunity to satisfy their curiosity, learn through play, and imagine their parents' childhood, fostering meaningful dialogue.

The walkable area of the interactive installation, marked on the floor with white lines, represents the physical footprint of the virtual room. This allows the user to move within both the virtual and real spaces in a corresponding manner.

Within the virtual space, objects are placed for the user to interact with.
The first category includes seven references to 1980s arcade video games, the second category consists of objects that can be interacted with physically, and the final category features musical objects.

The entire experience was created using Blender for modeling each individual element in the scene, while the interactive experience itself was fully developed in Unreal Engine. To track the user's movement within the space, an HTC Vive Tracker was attached to the bottom of the lightsaber, transmitting real-time data to Unreal Engine.

The object chosen to allow users to interact with the virtual world is a lightsaber, an iconic item from the cinematic world. Its length is a distinctive feature, making it possible for children to reach objects placed higher in the virtual world.

When the user interacts with one of the seven arcade game reference objects in the scene, an information panel opens, displaying a video of the game from that era and providing general information.
